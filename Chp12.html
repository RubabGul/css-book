Briards

A HEART WRAPPED IN FUR

The briard, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is the believed to have originated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd, and to protect sheep. The breed was
used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards
was used in th First World War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of briard is slowly recovering.
Charlemagne, Nepolean, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briards.
